About

I am an undergraduate computer science student at the University of Tennessee, in what I like to call "Year 4 out of 5". I began my undergraduate career as a pre-med Biomedical Engineering student, but after my sophomore year and taking my first coding class (EF 202, a course in MATLAB required for BME students), I discovered a passion for Computer Science and began anew as a CS major. I aspire to one day work for NASA on automation and guidance systems, powering humanity's next ventures off of this planet and into the stars beyond.

Currently I work in the Department of University Housing as a Resident Assistant. I've been in this role for 3 years now, and have been the RA for over 120 college freshman total. It's taught me a ton about working in collaboration with my peers, time management, dealing with conflict both personal and professional, and communicating with both peers and supervisors. It's been an invaluable experience and what I personally consider a highlight of my college career.
